Hello! I am a second year PhD Student at Carnegie Mellon's Language Technologies Institute advised by Louis-Philippe Morency in the MultiComp lab. I am interested in modeling social interaction using self-supervised and meta-learning methods.

My journey here was a winding one. I have worked as a branch manager for a U.S. Senate campaign, an aide to my state's other Senator, a Math teacher at a middle/high school in Costa Rica, a journalist at a daily newspaper in New Zealand, a cook at restaurants that have received the James Beard Award and Michelin one, two, and three star ratings, a software engineering intern at Uber, and as a research assistant with Professor Emily Mower Provost at the University of Michigan's CHAI Lab. I love hiking, singing, and cooking. "In the wild", I can be found attempting overly complex french recipes, marveling at the crafstmanship of a particularly well made piece of outerwear, or appreciating the perfect chocolate chip cookie.
